Why we start with dental / medspa

Best first niche

Dental & clinical aesthetics

High patient LTV, already spend on marketing, treat website as patient-acquisition asset not a brochure. Typical professional dental sites cost far more than $499.

Runner-up

Medspa / aesthetics

Premium brand sensitivity + high ticket treatments. Strong willingness to pay for a polished site that converts consults.

Later

Restaurants / gyms

Need websites, but often tighter budgets and slower decision cycles for a $499 one-time package.

What a dental website must do

  • Attract: local SEO / service pages for searches like dentist + city.
  • Convert: Book CTA, reviews, team, before/after, insurance clarity.
  • Capture after hours: online appointment request when phone is closed.
  • Reduce anxiety: real clinic photos, credentials, plain-language process.

Evidence snapshot (public market data)

  • Local service providers (dentists, clinics, consultants) are repeatedly listed among businesses that most need websites for credibility + local clients.
  • Dental website projects commonly price in the thousands ($3.5k–$9k+ range for quality builds) — our $499 sits as an easy “done-for-you preview → ownership” wedge.
  • Dental/medical site conversion averages around ~4%+ when the site is solid; practices already buy ads/SEO, so a better site multiplies existing spend.
  • Medspa custom sites are often positioned as premium growth assets ($7.5k–$30k narratives in industry guides) — again, willingness to pay is real.

Sources informing this pick: HostAdvice “businesses that need websites”, dental website cost/conversion guides (PatientGain, Rosemont), medspa website pricing guides, local-service website demand writeups.
